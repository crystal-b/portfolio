Prototype: Goal Tracker

For my Designing Meaningful Interactions class, I created a physical object that allows users to count up or down. The class received two user scenarios. One is for Rachel, who wiles away the days at work waiting for her next vacation. The other is for Jon, who is trying to cut back on his daily coffee consumption in order to sleep better. While both Rachel and Jon are essentially counting, I ended up thinking of this project more like goal tracking.

My initial sketch is based on Ikea's kitchen timer. I like the idea of twisting the object to get to a desired number. My next few sketches try to improve the abacus, the classic manual counting device. Some of my ideas are to make the tokens in different shapes and sizes based on row, to represent different values. This could help users with low or no visibility track their calculations easier. Another idea is to divide the two havles of the abacus by color, again to help keep track of calculations and which side is being counted.

The one requirement was that the object needed to let users count up to 100. But, Jon only need a limit of 15 cups per day while I made Rachel wait 93 days for her next trip. Because their needs are so disparate, my goal is to make the object customizable. The version I prototyped is a wall of tiles, which come in either 1" or 2" sizes. The larger tiles are for smaller goals.

The materials are paper, straws, glue and zip ties.
