Layout: Marketing Illustrations

Part of my job as a web content editor required image editing and resizing. When there was no usable photograph of a product, I constructed an image by assembling photos from multiple sources and drawing in any missing portions using some combination of Illustrator and Photoshop.

I created these supplemental images to both show alternate views of a product and highlight key features at a glance. Typically, product images failed to give users any indication of what they were looking at. I often knew the image was supposed to highlight something like an ice maker only by reading the file name, but the image itself was not shot to emphasize that particular feature.

Because the in-house content management system did not support captions, I decided to write brief headlines that users could quickly absorb. To keep the images from looking like the text was hastily layered on top, I also incorporated some graphic elements that stressed corresponding lines and angles between the text and image.
