Layout: Newspaper Project

Part of the training for my newspaper internship included designing pages on tight deadlines. One night, the other interns and I stayed up incredibly late waiting for the results of Wisconsin governor Scott Walkerâ€™s recall election. There was a gaping hole in the page on each of our screens because we were holding out for the final story. Walker won and we raced to print.

This version of the page was updated in December 2012 to include stories with better images.

I had four goals while creating this A1 page. One was to give it some life with vibrant colors. The second was to establish a clear hierarchy with headlines that varied in size, kerning and weight. The third was to write informative and captivating headlines. My final goal was to create a visual flow that would avoid the wall of text or layer cake effects and instead coerce the eye to make its way around the page.
